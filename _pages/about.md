Welcome! I am an Alexander von Humboldt (AvH) Postdoctoral Fellow at Karlsruhe Institute of Technology (KIT). My research aims at developing a new data analysis paradigm that is high-dimensional and unbinned using deep learning techniques.

Previously I was a graduate research & teaching assistant at Yale University and a research affiliate at Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory, where I developed deep learning-based algorithms for detector deconvolution/unfolding and applied them to performing precision measurements with the ATLAS collaboration at the Large Hadron Collider, which is an experiment that probes the smallest scales at the highest energies to explore the most fundamental questions of nature, such as baryongenesis, dark matter, and the beginning stage of our universe.
